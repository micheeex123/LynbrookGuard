Lynbrook Color Guard and Winter Guard

Color Guard vs Winter Guard

Color Guard is a performing arts sport that encorporates aspects of dance and equipment technique, often performed in conjunction with Lynbrook Marching Band. Taking place primarily during the fall sports season, which lasts from August to November, Lynbrook Color Guard enhances the visual aspect of the entire performance with the use of flags, rifles, sabres, and any other equipment (e.g., swing flags). Marching Band and Color Guard shows are typically around 8 minutes and performed outdoors on the football fields. This year, we will be competing in a variety of competitions as well as performing during football games (such as the Homecoming halftime show!).


Winter Guard, in contrast, is an indoor sport that takes place during the winter and spring sports season, which lasts from December to April, independent of the marching band. Winter Guard focuses much more on technique as well as intricate choreography and storytelling, occasionally utilizing special proprs or accessories to further enhance the show's visual appeal. In general, the main differences between Color Guard and Winter Guard are (1) the season in which they occur and (2) the presence, or lack thereof, of the marching band.
